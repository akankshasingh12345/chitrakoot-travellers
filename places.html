1. kamadgiri temple

Chitrakoot is a Gram Panchayat in the district of Madhya Pradesh. If you have read Ramayana, you must have come across this place there. It is not that the place has any special temple of Lord Rama, but there are a lot of places that could be related to the instances of Ramayana. This was a place where Lord Rama, Laxman and Sita visited during their exile. When you will visit this place, you will come to know about a lot of such things that are not even discussed in books. Apart from that, this place has a lot of historical places to visit. It is the part of Rewa, which itself is popular for being one of the most popular capitals of different kings. So, there are a lot of reasons for which one should visit the beautiful town of Chitrakoot. If you want to visit the places of religious significance here, then here is a list of some of the most important temples in Chitrakoot.

This temple is of utmost importance for Hindu pilgrims. It is said that this temple is located on a hill where Lord Rama, Laxman and Sita used to dwell, during their stay in the woods. The literal meaning of Kamadgiri is where all your desires get fulfilled. Well, that is probably one of the greatest reasons for which this temple has got so much crowd. There are other significant temples around and one can visit them also if they have come to this hill. Not just the religious significance, but the beauty of the place too attracts a lot of people to come here. The hill that is there in the background of the temple is lush green and that adds so much of beauty and charm to it. It is one of those sites that you must visit if you are in Chitrakoot.

2. Ram Darshan

Ram Darshan Temple, one of the most popular tourist attractions in Chitrakoot, offers much more than just a spiritual experience. The temple is common to other temples as far as the statues of Lord Rama, Goddess Sita and Lord Hanuman is concerned. What makes it different from the others is the complete prohibition on puja and offerings. Inside the temple, there is an artistic depiction of various events from the Hindu mythological epic, Ramayana. Various episodes are depicted in the form of statues, carvings and paintings. The intricate depictions which boast meticulous attention down to the last detail evoke nothing but admiration. In addition, there are also depictions of the Ramayana from the perspective of various countries like Indonesia, Thailand, Japan and Korea. These depictions also involve colorful masks and costumes which are used in those countries. Of particular interest to visitors to Ram Darshan Temple, counted amongst the most visited places in Chitrakoot, is the giant statue of Lord Hanuman. It occupies the entrance and blesses visitors at the entrance. Two other crowd favorites are sculptures of Goswami Tulsidas and Maharshi Valmiki. The temple itself is located close to River Mandakini, which lends it an air of serenity and solitude.

3. Gupta Godavari

This place is so popular and important from the religious point of view that it should be on the top of your list when visiting Chitrakoot. This is a chain of caves. These caves have water in them, but nobody has ever known how this water come to this place and how it stays there. This point alone makes this place so interesting to visit. There are a lot of myths related to this cave, but the one for which it is known among the entire Hindu community is that Lord Rama and Lord Laxman used to hold their court sessions here in this cave. You will see that there is a huge sculpture carved at the entrance of these caves. It is of Brahma, Vishnu, and Mahesh, the Tridev as they call it in Hindu mythology. You can visit this place easily by taking a bus. It is just 18km away from the Chitrakoot bus stop.

4. Sati Anusuya Temple

We know that this is one such name that you might have not heard, even if you belong to the Hindu community. However, those who know about Sati Anusuya had great faith in her. She is known as a lady with such powers that she can destroy the thing that tries to take the life of her husband. She had fought for her husband’s life, even from the supreme Gods. If we talk about Chitrakoot, then it is believed that it was Goddess Sati Ansuya who bought the existence of River Mandakini. This place is believed to be the home of Sati Ansuya. People say that she used to live here with her husband and sons. This is an ashram. Due to its large religious significance, this place is one of the most popular centers of attraction of Chitrakoot. It is located on the bank of Mandakini river and that kind of adds into its beauty and charm. If you want to visit the temple, you won’t be charged a single penny.

5. Bharat Milap Temple

If you know even a little bit about Ramayana, you must be aware of this entire instant. This instant refers to the time when the four brothers Rama, Laxman, Bharata, and Shatrughan met in the jungle. The incident is so popular that even now, when two people meet after a long time, then the meeting is referred to as Bharat Milap. Now, this temple is known as the place where the Bharat Milap took place. You will be glad to know that this temple is located on the hills of the Kamadgiri Temple. Just like the former one, this temple too is the picaresque location and attracts those too who don’t have any religious beliefs. It is said that if you come to this place, all your sins will get dissolved. The best time to come to this temple is during a festival that is held every year in the temple. Millions of devotees come to this place while the festival goes on.

6. Hanuman Dhara

Dhara in Hindi means the flow of water and Hanuman, as we know is one of the Gods of Hindus. The place is related to both of these things. This is a waterfall that is located on one of the hills of Chitrakoot. There are a lot of reasons for which this place is popular among tourists. It has a huge chain of temples and that is one of the biggest reasons for which Hindu devotees come to this place. It is said that Lord Rama had made this spring for Lord Hanuman when he came back after setting the entire Lanka on fire. It was made for his relaxation. You will have to ride 350 steps to reach the top of the hill, which makes it one of the good sites for trekking. This is another reason for which this place is popular. When you will reach the top of the place, you can see the entire town of Chitrakoot from there and the scene is breathtakingly beautiful.

7. Valmiki Ashram

Sage Valmiki is known for writing his version of Ramayana. Apart from that, there are a lot of other things for which he and his ashram is known. If we talk about the ashram today, then it is believed that this was the place he used to live in. It is located on one of the hills and is just a few kilometers away from the main town. It is one of the best tourist places in Chitrakoot and you could not afford to miss it at any cost. This place is also known as the birthplace of Lav and Kush, sons of Lord Rama and Goddess Sita. It is said that after Ram asked Sita to leave the place, influenced by a washerman, she came here and lived till the last day of her life. It is also important from a historical point of view.

8. Janaki Kund

Janaki is the alternate name of Goddess Sita. This Kund in Chitrakoot is very popular, as it is related to Goddess Sita. It is located near the Mandakini River. People say that this was Sita’s favorite spot to take a bath. You won’t be charged any money for visiting the Kund. The place could be seen crowded with Hindu pilgrims throughout the year. It is just two kilometers away from Ram Ghat. So, you can come here easily.

Janki Kund (a small tank or reservoir) is located about 2 km from Ram Ghat, another famous tourist attraction of Chitrakoot. Janki is another name for Goddess Sita and this place has been named after her. There are two temples located just behind the kund, namely, Sankat Mochan Hanuman Temple and Shree Raghuvir Temple. Across the kund lies the Pramod Van, whose presence plays a big role in enhancing the tranquillity of the place.

9. Sati Anusuya

Sati Anusuya Ashram is located about 18 km from Ramghat, a famous tourist attraction in Chitrakoot. It is one of the holiest places in the region and is considered a part of the "chitrakoot char dham". This place gets its name from the fact that it served as the ashram of Maharishi Atri along with his wife Mahasati Anasuya. She is also regarded as one of the mahasatis in holy scriptures of Hinduism because of her devout, pure and chaste character. There is also an interesting story behind her name. It is believed that this region was parched because of no rains for ten years. Then, Anasuya performed hard penance, and finally succeeded in bring River Mandakini down on earth. She was called Sati for her supreme sacrifice and enduring immense hardships during her penance. In fact, her dedication and love towards her husband and her duties were so great that even Goddess Sita sought her blessings during her exile with Lord Rama. She also received lessons in humility, devotion and all other qualities that a chaste, noble and devoted woman should possess.
